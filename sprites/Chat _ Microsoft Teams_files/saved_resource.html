Plan:

  1. Explain the rules of sudoku to all students.
  2. Ensure that all the students have a pencil or a pen.
  3. Take one of each sudoku and mark them with the number 1, get two more and mark them with the number 2. Repeat, incrementing the number by one for each pair of sudokus, until all sudokus have numbers on them.
  4. Divide the class alphabetically into two and give half the class the first sudoku and the other half the other sudoku. Give the sudokus out face down.
  5. Countdown from 3, telling the students to flip over the sudoku and start on go, then start the 4-minute timer.
  6. Tell the students to stop after 4 minutes, stop the timer and reset it.
  7. Give the students the sudokus with the same numbers as the sudokus they got last time. They should have a different sudoku, but the number on the back should be the same. Face down once again.
  8. Countdown from 3, telling the students to flip over and start the sudoku on go, then start the 4-minute timer. This time, also start playing the audio file that says the random numbers on the loudest volume you can manage to make sure everyone can hear it.
  9. if the audio file isnt playing over the classroom speakers, make sure to walk around the classroom so that all students can hear the distraction for equal time.
  10. Tell the students to stop after 4 minutes, stop the stopwatch and reset it, stop the audio file, and collect the sudokus from the students.
  11. Collect the data in a spreadsheet, making sure to pair the data of both sudokus that each student did.
